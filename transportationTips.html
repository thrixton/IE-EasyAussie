Types of Public Transportation in Melbourne

The public transportation system in Melbourne has rail, tram and bus services. This public transportation system provides the service to residents in Great Melbourne by over 300 bus routes and 16 main rail line. For these bus service, some quite special community or university bus services are operated without any charging. Therefore, the free shuttle bus is free for student and this is also very important consideration for student choosing their ideal living location.


Tickets


The ticketing system of public transportation in Melbourne is according to the “Zones”. Because of the large size of the Great Melbourne, the different areas are divided into “Zones” in Melbourne, which include:

  • Free Tram Zone (applies to trams only)
  • Zone 1
  • Zone 2
  • The Zone 1/2 boundary overlap
more relevant information can be found in

https://www.ptv.vic.gov.au/tickets/fares/zones/


Myki Card


Myki card is the reusable smart card by using in Melbourne’s public transportation. It is very easy to buy, top up and use in Melbourne. Station ticket office, Myki machine and most convince shores have the top up service. Even on the bus, bus driver can top up your Myki card. The easiest way of using of Myki card is touching on and off. Passengers touch on the sensor machine when they board the public transportation and touch off when they disembark. More relevant information can be found in

https://www.ptv.vic.gov.au/tickets/myki/

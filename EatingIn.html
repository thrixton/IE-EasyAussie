Student living --- Few Tips for Eating at home

Some small tips for student to saving the money on food. Small savings add up quickly, whether you want to stretch your budget further or just want to save money for something special.


Saving Money On Eating Home


Cooking and eating at home can saving 50% and sometimes even 80% rather than eating out. Therefore, if you can cook and eat at home, you can save a lot of budget on eating. If you don’t know how to cook, it is time to learn. You can download some online recipes or learning from your friends and work out your favorites. You can cook a little bit more as your nest day’s lunch. These meals you cooked always much cheaper than eating out or takeaway. Some useful online recipes in or https://www.bbcgoodfood.com/


Cheap Shopping For Food


Choose Aldi’s

Coles and Woolworths are the two major supermarkets in Australian, but they are not the cheapest supermarkets. The cheapest supermarket in Austrian is Aldi’s. The cheap level of supermarkets is followed by Aldi’s, Coles and Woolworths and then expensive one is IGA. The different of supermarket on grocery and food expense can be 40% cost difference. You can find your local nearest Aldi’s here

“Reduced” Items

Sometimes stores will provide some “reduced” perishable products such as meat, deli, bake, fruit and vegetable. These “reduced” products can be half price or sometimes will be less than half price. These item most will be provided at the end of the day, so this is the good time to shopping.

Online Shopping and Free Delivery

Shopping in local supermarket is easiest way to buy what you need. However, if student is not convenient to get there, ordering online is the best way to get free delivery and convenient service. The online order can help student plan more careful and easily find the best discount in that week.
